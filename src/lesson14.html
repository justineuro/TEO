LESSON 14.
Re-, -ad-, ek-.

The prefixes re- and ek- and the suffix -ad- are attached to verbs.

Re- has nearly the same meaning as in English, back or again, as re-pagi, to pay back; re-porti, to carry back; re-Äµeti, to throw back; re-salti, to rebound; re-kanti to sing again; re-legi, to read over again.

-ad- denotes the continuance or continued repetition of an action; it means goes on doing, or keeps on doing, or is in the habit of, or in the past used to, as spiri, to breathe, spirado, respiration; movi, to move, movado, continued movement; fumi, to smoke, fumado, the habit of smoking; aÅ­di, to hear, aÅ­dado, the sense of hearing.

Ek- has the opposite meaning to -ad-; it signifies the beginning of an action, or a short or sudden action, as kanti, to sing, ekkanti, to begin to sing; ridi, to laugh, ekridi, to burst out laughing; krii, to cry or call, ekkrii, to cry out; iri, to go, ekiri, to set out; dormi, to sleep, ekdormi, to fall asleep.
